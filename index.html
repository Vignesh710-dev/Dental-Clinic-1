Dental Tips & Education

Brush your teeth for two minutes, twice a day using fluoride toothpaste. Hold your brush at a 45-degree angle to your gums and use short, gentle strokes. Don't forget to brush your tongue to remove bacteria and freshen breath.

Flossing once a day removes plaque and food particles from between teeth where your toothbrush can't reach. Gently curve the floss around each tooth and move it up and down against the tooth surface and under the gumline.

Limit sugary foods and drinks as they contribute to tooth decay. Choose tooth-friendly foods like fruits, vegetables, cheese, and nuts. Drink plenty of water, especially after meals, to help wash away food particles and bacteria.

Visit your dentist every six months for professional cleaning and check-up. Regular dental visits help detect problems early when they're easier and less expensive to treat. Don't wait until you have pain to see a dentist.
